# Professor Audit
Nobody Teaches You How to Work a Workpaper

I spent years in governmental audit. One thing I noticed early: there's a gap between what school teaches and what the job expects.

Universities teach you accounting theory. They cover the standards — GASB, FASB, AICPA. You pass the CPA exam. Then you show up to your first engagement and someone hands you a trial balance, a general ledger, and a chart of accounts. They say: "Work the capital assets."

And you think: work it how, exactly?

Nobody walks you through the process of arriving at a conclusion. How to take source data, apply the right procedures under the right standards framework, and produce a workpaper that says "Unmodified," "Modified," or "Disclaimer of Opinion" — and why.

You're expected to learn by watching. By making mistakes that get caught in review. By hoping the senior on your engagement has time to teach you. Sometimes they do. Often they don't.

# The gap
There are a lot of people who want to be auditors but don't know how to be auditors. Not because they lack intelligence or ambition. Because nobody gave them a way to practice the actual work.
Think about it: a pilot doesn't learn to fly by reading about aerodynamics and then climbing into a commercial jet. They use a simulator. Repetition with real scenarios in a controlled environment.
Auditors don't have that. Until now.

# What I'm building?

I'm building a tool called Teresa.

Teresa is an AI-powered application that generates real benchmark workpapers. You pick a standards framework — GASB, FASB, or AICPA. You pick an account series. You use sample data or upload your own. Teresa walks through the audit procedures and produces a complete workpaper with a conclusion.
It's not a course. It's not a textbook. It's a practice environment grounded in authoritative standards — GAO Yellow Book, GASB 34, 68, 75, 87, 96, FASB/ASC, and AICPA.

Your first three uses are free. Nothing is stored. No accounts, no data retention.
Who this is for

If any of this sounds familiar, Teresa might be for you:

- You're studying for the CPA exam and want to understand what the job actually looks like.
- You're a new staff auditor and wish you had a way to check your own work before the senior reviews it.
- You're interested in governmental audit but don't have a mentor to learn from.
- You switched into audit from another area of accounting and need to get up to speed fast.
